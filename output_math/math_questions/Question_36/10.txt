Question: Let $\theta$ be an acute angle such that_x000D_
\[\sin 5 \theta = \sin^5 \theta.\]Compute $\tan 2 \theta.$
3o-mini: We are given that θ is an acute angle satisfying

  sin(5θ) = [sin θ]⁵.

Step 1. Express sin(5θ) in terms of sin θ using a multiple-angle formula:
  sin(5θ) = 16 sin⁵θ − 20 sin³θ + 5 sin θ.

Setting this equal to sin⁵θ gives:
  16 sin⁵θ − 20 sin³θ + 5 sin θ = sin⁵θ.

Step 2. Rearrange and factor:
  16 sin⁵θ − sin⁵θ − 20 sin³θ + 5 sin θ = 0  
  15 sin⁵θ − 20 sin³θ + 5 sin θ = 0  
  5 sin θ (3 sin⁴θ − 4 sin²θ + 1) = 0.

Since θ is acute, sin θ ≠ 0. Therefore, we set the factor in parentheses equal to zero:
  3 sin⁴θ − 4 sin²θ + 1 = 0.

Step 3. Let x = sin²θ. Then, the equation becomes:
  3 x² − 4x + 1 = 0.

Solve this quadratic using the quadratic formula:
  x = [4 ± √(16 − 12)] / (2×3)  
  x = [4 ± 2] / 6.

This gives us two solutions:
  x = (4 + 2) / 6 = 1  or  x = (4 − 2) / 6 = 1/3.

Since sin²θ = 1 would lead to sin θ = 1 (θ = 90°), which is not considered acute, we accept sin²θ = 1/3. Thus,
  sin θ = 1/√3.

Step 4. Calculate cos θ:
  cos θ = √(1 − sin²θ) = √(1 − 1/3) = √(2/3) = (√2)/(√3).

Step 5. Find tan θ:
  tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = (1/√3) / (√2/√3) = 1/√2.

Step 6. Use the double-angle formula for tangent:
  tan(2θ) = (2 tan θ) / (1 − tan²θ).

Substitute tan θ = 1/√2:
  tan(2θ) = [2(1/√2)] / [1 − (1/2)]  
  tan(2θ) = (2/√2) / (1/2)  
  tan(2θ) = (√2) / (1/2)  
  tan(2θ) = 2√2.

Thus, the value of tan 2θ is 2√2.
