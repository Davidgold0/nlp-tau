Question: Find the area of a triangle with side lengths 8, 9, and 9.
Teacher: Let's begin by understanding what the problem is asking. We have a triangle with side lengths 8, 9, and 9, and we need to find its area. A common way to solve a problem like this is to use Heron’s formula. Here’s a quick outline of the steps involved:

1. Calculate the semi-perimeter (s) of the triangle using the formula:  
  s = (a + b + c) / 2  
  where a, b, and c are the side lengths.
2. Use Heron’s formula to compute the area:  
  Area = √[s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c)].

Let’s start with the first step. Can you calculate the semi-perimeter (s) for this triangle? Please show your work for adding the side lengths and dividing by 2.
Student: Sure! To calculate the semi-perimeter (s) of the triangle with side lengths 8, 9, and 9, I will use the formula:

\[ s = \frac{a + b + c}{2} \]

First, I will add the side lengths together:

\[ 8 + 9 + 9 = 26 \]

Now, I will divide this sum by 2 to find the semi-perimeter:

\[ s = \frac{26}{2} = 13 \]

So, the semi-perimeter (s) is 13. What's the next step?
Teacher: Great job calculating the semi-perimeter as 13! Now let's move to the next step. We need to use Heron’s formula to find the area of the triangle. Heron’s formula states that:

  Area = √[s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c)]

With s = 13 and the side lengths a = 8, b = 9, and c = 9, can you calculate the product inside the square root, that is, s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c)? Please show your work step by step.

1. Calculate \( s - a \):
   \[
   s - a = 13 - 8 = 5

\[
s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c) = 13 \times 5 \times 4 \times 4
\]
