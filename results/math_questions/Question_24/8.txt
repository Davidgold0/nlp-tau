Question: There exist constants $c_2,$ $c_1,$ and $c_0$ such that_x000D_
\[x^3 + x^2 - 5 = (x - 3)^3 + c_2 (x - 3)^2 + c_1 (x - 3) + c_0.\]Find $c_2^2 + c_1^2 + c_0^2.$
3o-mini: We are given

  x³ + x² – 5 = (x – 3)³ + c₂ (x – 3)² + c₁ (x – 3) + c₀.

Step 1. Expand (x – 3)³:
  (x – 3)³ = x³ – 9x² + 27x – 27.

Step 2. Expand c₂ (x – 3)²:
  (x – 3)² = x² – 6x + 9,
  so c₂ (x – 3)² = c₂ x² – 6c₂ x + 9c₂.

Step 3. Expand c₁ (x – 3):
  c₁ (x – 3) = c₁ x – 3c₁.

Step 4. Now, combine all terms:
  (x – 3)³ + c₂ (x – 3)² + c₁ (x – 3) + c₀ =
   = x³ – 9x² + 27x – 27 + c₂ x² – 6c₂ x + 9c₂ + c₁ x – 3c₁ + c₀.

Group like powers of x:
  Coefficient of x³: 1.
  Coefficient of x²: (–9 + c₂).
  Coefficient of x: (27 – 6c₂ + c₁).
  Constant term: (–27 + 9c₂ – 3c₁ + c₀).

Step 5. Equate coefficients with the given polynomial x³ + x² – 5.

For x³: 1 = 1    (No condition here.)

For x²: 
  –9 + c₂ = 1  ⟹ c₂ = 10.

For x:
  27 – 6c₂ + c₁ = 0.
Substitute c₂ = 10:
  27 – 60 + c₁ = 0  ⟹ c₁ = 33.

For the constant term:
  –27 + 9c₂ – 3c₁ + c₀ = –5.
Substitute c₂ = 10 and c₁ = 33:
  –27 + 90 – 99 + c₀ = –5,
  –36 + c₀ = –5  ⟹ c₀ = 31.

Step 6. Compute c₂² + c₁² + c₀²:
  c₂² = 10² = 100,
  c₁² = 33² = 1089,
  c₀² = 31² = 961.
Thus, the sum is 100 + 1089 + 961 = 2150.

The final answer is 2150.
