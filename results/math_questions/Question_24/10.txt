Question: There exist constants $c_2,$ $c_1,$ and $c_0$ such that_x000D_
\[x^3 + x^2 - 5 = (x - 3)^3 + c_2 (x - 3)^2 + c_1 (x - 3) + c_0.\]Find $c_2^2 + c_1^2 + c_0^2.$
3o-mini: We are given the equation

  x³ + x² – 5 = (x – 3)³ + c₂(x – 3)² + c₁(x – 3) + c₀.

Our goal is to determine the constants c₂, c₁, and c₀ and then compute c₂² + c₁² + c₀².

Step 1. Expand the right-hand side.

First, expand (x – 3)³:
  (x – 3)³ = x³ – 3·x²·3 + 3·x·9 – 27 = x³ – 9x² + 27x – 27.

Next, expand c₂(x – 3)². Since
  (x – 3)² = x² – 6x + 9,
we have:
  c₂(x – 3)² = c₂x² – 6c₂x + 9c₂.

Similarly, expand c₁(x – 3):
  c₁(x – 3) = c₁x – 3c₁.

Now, combine all terms:
  (x – 3)³ + c₂(x – 3)² + c₁(x – 3) + c₀
  = [x³ – 9x² + 27x – 27] + [c₂x² – 6c₂x + 9c₂] + [c₁x – 3c₁] + c₀.

Step 2. Combine like terms.

Group the coefficients for x³, x², x, and the constant:
 • x³: Coefficient = 1.
 • x²: Coefficient = (–9 + c₂).
 • x: Coefficient = (27 – 6c₂ + c₁).
 • Constant term: Coefficient = (–27 + 9c₂ – 3c₁ + c₀).

Step 3. Equate coefficients with the left-hand side.

The left-hand side is:
  x³ + x² + 0x – 5.
Setting the coefficients equal gives us:

1. For x³: 1 = 1 (already satisfied).

2. For x²:
  –9 + c₂ = 1 ⟹ c₂ = 1 + 9 = 10.

3. For x:
  27 – 6c₂ + c₁ = 0.
Substitute c₂ = 10:
  27 – 60 + c₁ = 0 ⟹ –33 + c₁ = 0 ⟹ c₁ = 33.

4. For the constant term:
  –27 + 9c₂ – 3c₁ + c₀ = –5.
Substitute c₂ = 10 and c₁ = 33:
  –27 + 90 – 99 + c₀ = –5 ⟹ (–27 + 90 = 63, 63 – 99 = –36) ⟹ –36 + c₀ = –5.
So, c₀ = –5 + 36 = 31.

Step 4. Compute c₂² + c₁² + c₀².

  c₂² = 10² = 100,
  c₁² = 33² = 1089,
  c₀² = 31² = 961.

Adding them together:
  100 + 1089 + 961 = 2150.

Thus, the value of c₂² + c₁² + c₀² is 2150.
